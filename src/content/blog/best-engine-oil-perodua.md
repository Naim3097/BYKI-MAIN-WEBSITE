## Why 0W-20 for Newer Perodua Models?

All Perodua models from 2018 onwards use Daihatsu/Toyota NR-series engines designed for ultra-low viscosity oil:

### Engineering reasons:
1. **Tighter engine clearances** — precision-machined bearings and pistons have smaller gaps, requiring thinner oil
2. **Fuel economy standards** — EEV (Energy Efficient Vehicle) compliance requires reduced friction
3. **D-CVT compatibility** — the engine and transmission work as a system; the engine oil viscosity affects overall efficiency
4. **VVT-i response** — variable valve timing solenoids need fast-flowing oil

### What happens if you use 5W-30 in a 0W-20 Perodua:
- Fuel consumption increases 1–3%
- VVT-i response slows, reducing performance
- Oil pump works harder (marginal stress)
- **Warranty may be voided** if engine issues arise during the warranty period

## Perodua Service Centre vs Independent Workshop

### Perodua Service Centre
- Uses Petronas Syntium products (matched to each model)
- **Cost:** RM 150–250 (oil + filter + inspection for 0W-20 models)
- Maintains official service record (important for warranty and resale)
- GearUp packages available with fixed pricing

### Independent Workshop (Bengkel)
- **Cost:** RM 100–200 (same service, potentially cheaper)
- Must supply or verify correct oil grade
- No official service record stamp (use receipt as alternative)
- **Risk:** Some workshops default to 5W-30 or 10W-40 for all cars

### Best practice:
- Service at Perodua during warranty (first 5 years / 150,000 km)
- After warranty, independent workshops are fine IF you verify the oil grade
- Bring your own oil if uncertain — most workshops charge RM 20–40 labour for customer-supplied oil

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I use Toyota oil in my Perodua?
A: Yes. Perodua engines are Toyota/Daihatsu designs. Toyota Genuine Motor Oil 0W-20 is fully compatible with any Perodua model that specifies 0W-20. They meet the same ILSAC GF-6A and API SP standards.

### Q: Is Petronas Syntium 7000 better than Shell Helix Ultra for Perodua?
A: Both are excellent 0W-20 full synthetic oils meeting the same API/ILSAC standards. Petronas Syntium 7000 is Perodua's official recommendation and is what their service centres use, so it's the safest choice. Shell Helix Ultra 0W-20 is an equally good alternative.

### Q: My Ativa is turbo — shouldn't it use thicker oil?
A: No. The Ativa's 1KR-VET turbo engine is specifically designed for 0W-20. The turbo bearing and oil cooling system are engineered for this viscosity. Using 5W-30 or 5W-40 won't "help" the turbo — it'll reduce fuel efficiency and potentially affect VVT-i performance.

### Q: My workshop says 10W-40 is "more suitable for Malaysian weather"
A: This advice is outdated. 10W-40 is not suitable for any Perodua model that specifies 0W-20. The "hot weather needs thick oil" myth doesn't apply because engine operating temperature (90–110°C) is the same regardless of outside temperature. Your engine isn't getting hotter in Malaysian weather — only the ambient temperature is higher, and 0W-20 handles that perfectly.

### Q: How do I know if my workshop used the correct oil?
A: Ask to see the oil bottle before they pour it. Check: (1) Correct viscosity on the label (0W-20 or 5W-30), (2) Full synthetic or semi-synthetic as required, (3) API SP or SN rating visible. After the change, check your RPM at idle with BYKI Live Data — it should be smooth at 650–750 RPM.
