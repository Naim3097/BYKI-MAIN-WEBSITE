# Why Your Car Won't Start — 12 Common Causes & Fixes

> **Quick Answer:** A car that won't start is usually caused by a dead battery, faulty starter motor, or fuel delivery problem. If you hear clicking but the engine won't turn over, the battery is the most likely culprit. If the engine cranks but won't fire, suspect fuel or ignition issues.

You turn the key (or press the button), and nothing happens — or the engine cranks weakly but refuses to start. It's one of the most stressful car problems, especially when you're stuck in a parking lot in the Malaysian heat.

The good news: most no-start conditions have straightforward causes. This guide covers the 12 most common reasons your car won't start, how to diagnose each one, and what it typically costs to fix in Malaysia.

## Step 1: What Happens When You Try to Start?

Before diving into causes, pay attention to what your car does when you try to start it. This narrows the diagnosis immediately.

| What You Experience | Most Likely Cause |
|---|---|
| Nothing at all — no sound, no lights | Dead battery, loose battery terminal, blown fuse |
| Clicking sound, engine won't turn | Weak battery, bad starter motor, corroded terminals |
| Engine cranks normally but won't fire | Fuel pump, fuel filter, ignition coil, spark plugs |
| Engine starts briefly then dies | Fuel delivery issue, idle air control, immobiliser |
| Grinding noise when starting | Starter motor gear damage |
| All lights work but no crank | Starter relay, neutral safety switch, immobiliser |

## Dead or Weak Battery

**The #1 cause of a car that won't start.** Your battery provides the electrical energy needed to crank the engine. In Malaysia's heat (35–40°C regularly), car batteries degrade faster than in cooler climates — a battery that lasts 5 years in Europe may only last 2–3 years here.

### Symptoms of a dead battery
- No response when turning the key
- Dashboard lights flicker or are dim
- Clicking sound from the starter area
- Accessories (radio, lights) work weakly or not at all
- Car started fine yesterday but won't today

### Why it happens
- **Age** — Malaysian heat accelerates chemical degradation. Most batteries need replacement every 18–30 months
- **Parasitic drain** — something drawing power while the car is off (dashcam, aftermarket alarm, faulty module)
- **Short trips** — frequent short drives don't give the alternator enough time to fully recharge the battery
- **Leaving accessories on** — interior light, headlights, or USB charger left overnight
- **Corroded terminals** — white or green buildup on battery posts prevents proper connection

### What to do
1. Try jump-starting with cables from another car
2. Check battery terminals for corrosion — clean with a wire brush
3. Have the battery tested at any workshop or battery shop (usually free)
4. If the battery is older than 2 years and keeps dying, replace it

## Faulty Starter Motor

The starter motor is an electric motor that physically turns (cranks) the engine to get it running. When it fails, you'll hear a clicking sound or a grinding noise, but the engine won't crank.

### Symptoms
- Single loud click when turning the key, then nothing
- Grinding or whirring noise without engine cranking
- Intermittent starting — works sometimes, fails other times
- Starter engages slowly, engine cranks sluggishly even with a good battery

### Common in
Proton Saga, Proton Persona, older Toyota Vios — especially vehicles past 100,000 km.

## Fuel Delivery Problems

If the engine cranks normally (you hear it turning) but won't fire up, fuel may not be reaching the cylinders.

### Fuel pump failure
The fuel pump sits inside the fuel tank and pushes petrol to the engine. When it fails, the engine gets no fuel. Listen for a brief humming sound from the rear of the car when you turn the key to the ON position — that's the fuel pump priming. No sound means possible pump failure.

### Clogged fuel filter
A blocked fuel filter starves the engine of fuel. Common in cars past 80,000 km that have never had the filter replaced.

### Empty tank or fuel gauge error
It sounds obvious, but a faulty fuel gauge can show more fuel than is actually in the tank. This is surprisingly common in older Perodua Myvi and Proton models.

## Ignition System Failures

The ignition system creates the spark that ignites the fuel-air mixture. If there's no spark, the engine will crank but won't start.

### Faulty spark plugs
Worn or fouled spark plugs can't create a strong enough spark. In Malaysia, spark plugs should be inspected every 20,000 km and replaced by 40,000–60,000 km depending on the type (copper, iridium, or platinum).

### Ignition coil failure
The ignition coil converts battery voltage into the high voltage needed to fire the spark plugs. Modern cars have individual coils per cylinder (coil-on-plug). A single failed coil may cause misfires rather than a complete no-start — but multiple coil failures will prevent starting.

### Ignition switch fault
The ignition switch itself can wear out, especially in older cars. Symptoms include intermittent starting, accessories not powering on, or the key turning but nothing happening.

## Sensor and Electrical Faults

Modern engines rely on sensors to calculate fuel injection timing, air intake, and ignition timing. If a critical sensor fails, the engine may not start.

### Crankshaft position sensor (CKP)
This tells the ECU the position of the crankshaft. Without it, the ECU doesn't know when to fire the spark plugs or inject fuel. A failed CKP sensor is one of the most common no-start causes in modern Malaysian cars. Common fault codes: **P0335**, **P0336**.

### Camshaft position sensor (CMP)
Works with the crankshaft sensor to determine valve timing. Failure can prevent starting or cause very rough running. Common fault codes: **P0340**, **P0341**.

### Immobiliser / key transponder
Your car key has a transponder chip that communicates with the immobiliser system. If the signal isn't recognised (dead key battery, damaged chip, or system malfunction), the engine won't start as an anti-theft measure.

### Blown fuse or relay
A blown fuse in the fuel pump circuit, ignition circuit, or ECU relay can prevent starting. Check the fuse box — your owner's manual shows which fuse controls what.

## Other Causes

### Timing belt/chain failure
If the timing belt snaps, the engine cannot start and may suffer severe internal damage. This is catastrophic in interference engines (most Honda, some Toyota). Timing belts should be replaced at the manufacturer's interval — typically 80,000–100,000 km.

### Flooded engine
If you crank the engine repeatedly without it starting, excess fuel can flood the cylinders. Wait 10–15 minutes, then try again with the accelerator fully pressed while cranking (this opens the throttle to clear excess fuel).

### Water ingestion
After heavy rain or driving through floodwater (common during Malaysia's monsoon season), water can enter the air intake and prevent combustion. **Never try to start a car that has been submerged** — this can cause hydrostatic lock and destroy the engine.

## Prevention Tips

1. **Replace your battery proactively** — don't wait for it to die completely. After 2 years in Malaysia's climate, have it tested every 6 months
2. **Drive for at least 20 minutes** periodically to keep the battery charged, especially if you mostly make short trips
3. **Check battery terminals** every service — clean any corrosion with baking soda and water
4. **Replace spark plugs on schedule** — every 40,000 km for copper, 80,000 km for iridium
5. **Don't ignore the check engine light** — it may indicate a sensor that's degrading before it fails completely
6. **Keep fuel above quarter tank** — running low strains the fuel pump and increases sediment pickup

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Why won't my car start but the battery is fine?
A: If the battery has good voltage but the car won't start, check the starter motor, fuel pump, crankshaft position sensor, or immobiliser system. A BYKI scan can reveal stored fault codes that point to the exact cause. The starter motor is the most common culprit if you hear a single click.

### Q: Can a car not start because of an alternator?
A: Indirectly, yes. A failing alternator won't charge the battery while driving, so the battery gradually drains. If the alternator has been failing for a few days, the battery may not have enough charge to start the engine. Check battery voltage — below 12.4V at rest suggests insufficient charge.

### Q: Why does my car not start in the morning?
A: Cold starts are harder on the battery because chemical reactions inside the battery slow down at lower temperatures. In Malaysia, morning temperatures are generally mild, so a morning no-start usually indicates a weakened battery, parasitic drain (something drawing power overnight), or a sensor that performs worse when cold.

### Q: How much does it cost to diagnose a no-start at a workshop in Malaysia?
A: Most independent workshops (bengkel) in Malaysia charge RM 30–80 for a diagnostic scan. Some workshops offer free scans if you proceed with the repair. With BYKI, you can perform the same diagnostic scan yourself for free using a Bluetooth OBD-II adapter (RM 50–150 one-time purchase).

### Q: My car clicks but won't start — is it the battery or starter?
A: Rapid clicking (multiple clicks) usually means a weak battery that can't deliver enough current. A single loud click followed by silence usually indicates a faulty starter motor or starter solenoid. Try jump-starting the car — if it starts, the issue is the battery. If jump-starting doesn't help, the starter likely needs replacement.
