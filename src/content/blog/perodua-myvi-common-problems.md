# Perodua Myvi Common Problems & How to Diagnose Them

> **Quick Answer:** The Perodua Myvi is Malaysia's best-selling car — reliable and affordable, but not problem-free. Common issues include CVT judder, throttle body fouling, aircon compressor failure, engine mounting wear, and power window failures. Most problems are manageable with early detection. If you own a Myvi, knowing these common issues can save you thousands in preventive maintenance.

## Common Problems by Generation

### 3rd Gen Myvi (2018–Present) — D-CVT Era

#### 1. CVT judder / shudder during low-speed crawling
**What it feels like:** A vibration or shudder when moving slowly in traffic (10–30 km/h), especially on slight inclines or when the A/C is on.

**Why it happens:** The D-CVT (Dual-mode CVT) uses a split-gear mechanism at low speeds. The transition between gear drive and belt drive can cause judder if the CVT fluid degrades or the system calibration is off.

**How common:** Very common — this is the #1 complaint from 3rd-gen Myvi owners on Malaysian forums (Paultan.org, Lowyat, Facebook groups).

**Fix:**
- CVT fluid change (every 20,000–30,000 km in Malaysian conditions)
- CVT fluid flush + Perodua-specific CVT fluid only
- In severe cases, CVT valve body replacement (RM 1,500–3,000)

**Cost:** RM 200–400 for CVT fluid change, RM 1,500–3,000 for valve body

#### 2. Throttle body carbon buildup
**What it feels like:** Rough idle, RPM fluctuations, slight hesitation when pulling away from stops.

**Why it happens:** Malaysia's stop-start driving conditions cause carbon deposits to accumulate on the throttle plate and bore. The electronic throttle body (no cable) is sensitive to buildup — even a thin layer affects airflow.

**How common:** Very common after 30,000–50,000 km, especially in KL/PJ/Shah Alam traffic driving.

**Fix:** Throttle body cleaning (RM 50–100 at a workshop, or DIY with throttle body cleaner)

**Diagnosis with BYKI:** Check for **P0505** (Idle Air Control malfunction) or **P2111/P2112** (throttle actuator codes). Live Data will show unstable idle RPM (jumping between 500–1,000 RPM instead of steady 700 RPM).

#### 3. Engine mounting wear
**What it feels like:** Noticeable vibration through the steering wheel and cabin at idle, especially with A/C on. The vibration reduces when driving.

**Why it happens:** Engine mountings (also called engine mounts) dampen vibration between the engine and chassis. They're made of rubber that deteriorates in heat — Malaysia's constant 30–35°C accelerates this degradation. The 1.3L Myvi is a 3-cylinder engine (well, technically the 1NR is 4-cylinder, but runs at low RPM where vibrations are more noticeable).

**How common:** Common after 3–5 years / 60,000–100,000 km.

**Cost:** RM 150–400 per mounting (usually 2–3 need replacement), total RM 400–1,000 including labour.

#### 4. Aircon compressor noise/failure
**What it feels like:** Clicking, rattling, or grinding noise from the engine bay when A/C is on. Eventually, the A/C stops blowing cold air.

**Why it happens:** Malaysian A/C systems work extremely hard — the climate demands constant A/C usage (unlike temperate countries where A/C is seasonal). The compressor clutch and internal bearings wear out.

**How common:** Moderately common after 5–7 years. Perodua uses Denso compressors, which are reliable but not immortal under constant tropical use.

**Cost:** RM 800–1,500 for compressor replacement (including gas recharge and labour).

#### 5. Power window regulator failure
**What it feels like:** Window moves slowly, makes grinding noises, or stops working entirely. Often happens one window at a time.

**Why it happens:** The plastic regulator gear teeth wear down, or the electric motor burns out. This is a known Myvi issue across all generations.

**Cost:** RM 150–350 per window.

### 2nd Gen Myvi (2011–2017) — The Workhorse

#### 1. EGR valve carbon buildup
**What it feels like:** Rough idle, hesitation, occasional check engine light.

**Why:** The EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation) valve recirculates exhaust gas into the intake manifold, but carbon deposits clog it over time.

**Fix:** EGR cleaning (RM 100–200) or replacement (RM 300–600).

**BYKI code:** P0401 (EGR Insufficient Flow)

#### 2. Oil leaks from valve cover gasket
**What it feels like:** Burning oil smell, oil drips on the engine block, visible wetness around the valve cover.

**Why:** The valve cover gasket is rubber that hardens and shrinks with heat cycling. Malaysian heat accelerates this process.

**Cost:** RM 80–200 (gasket + labour)

#### 3. Catalytic converter degradation
**What it feels like:** Check engine light (P0420), reduced power, slight sulfur smell.

**Why:** After 100,000+ km, the catalytic converter's internal substrate begins to break down. Malaysian fuel quality (RON 95) contributes to slightly faster degradation compared to higher-quality fuels.

**Cost:** RM 600–1,500 depending on OEM vs aftermarket.

**BYKI code:** P0420 (Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold)

#### 4. Starter motor failure
**What it feels like:** Clicking sound when turning the key, car doesn't crank.

**Cost:** RM 250–500 for starter motor replacement.

### 1st Gen Myvi (2005–2011) — The Pioneer

#### 1. 4-speed automatic transmission issues
**What it feels like:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.

**Why:** The 4-speed auto (not CVT) uses a traditional torque converter. At 100,000+ km, internal clutch packs and solenoids wear.

**Fix:** ATF change (RM 100–200) first. If that doesn't help, transmission repair/overhaul (RM 1,500–3,500).

#### 2. Ignition coil failure
**What it feels like:** Misfiring, engine shaking, check engine light.

**Why:** The 1st gen uses a coil-on-plug system. Coils degrade with age and heat exposure.

**BYKI codes:** P0300 (Random Misfire), P0301–P0304 (Specific Cylinder Misfires)

**Cost:** RM 50–120 per coil. Replace all 4 at once if one fails.

#### 3. Radiator leak
**What it feels like:** Overheating, coolant level dropping, sweet smell from engine bay.

**Why:** The plastic-and-aluminium radiator develops cracks at the plastic tank seams after 8–10 years.

**Cost:** RM 200–500 for radiator replacement.

## How to Diagnose Myvi Problems With BYKI

The Perodua Myvi supports OBD-II diagnostics. Here's how to use BYKI:

### Step 1: Connect
Plug a Bluetooth OBD-II adapter into the Myvi's OBD-II port (located under the dashboard, driver's side, to the right of the steering column).

### Step 2: Open BYKI
Go to [BYKI](https://www.bykiofficial.com/login) on your phone browser.

### Step 3: Health Scan
Run a Health Scan to check for stored and pending fault codes. Common codes on the Myvi:

| Code | What It Means | Common Cause |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| P0420 | Catalyst efficiency low | Aging catalytic converter |
| P0505 | Idle air control malfunction | Dirty throttle body |
| P0171 | System too lean | Vacuum leak, dirty MAF sensor |
| P0300–P0304 | Misfires | Spark plugs, ignition coils |
| P0401 | EGR insufficient flow | Carbon-clogged EGR |
| P0128 | Thermostat below temp | Stuck-open thermostat |

### Step 4: Live Data
Monitor these key parameters:

| Parameter | Normal Range | What Abnormal Means |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Coolant temp | 85–100°C | Thermostat or fan issue |
| Intake air temp | 30–50°C (Malaysia) | MAF/IAT sensor problem |
| RPM at idle | 650–750 RPM (A/C off) | Throttle body, vacuum leak |
| Short-term fuel trim | -10% to +10% | Lean/rich issue |
| Long-term fuel trim | -5% to +5% | Persistent mixture issue |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is the Perodua Myvi a reliable car?
A: Yes — the Myvi is one of the most reliable cars sold in Malaysia. Its Toyota/Daihatsu-derived engine platform has a strong track record. Most problems are wear-and-tear items, not design flaws. With proper maintenance, Myvi engines comfortably reach 200,000+ km.

### Q: Should I worry about the CVT?
A: The D-CVT in the 3rd gen Myvi is generally reliable IF the CVT fluid is changed regularly (every 20,000–30,000 km). Most CVT problems stem from neglected fluid changes. Don't follow the "lifetime fluid" myth — there's no such thing in Malaysian conditions.

### Q: Perodua service centre or independent workshop?
A: For warranty-period servicing, stick with Perodua. After warranty expires, a good independent workshop can do the same work for 20–40% less. The key is ensuring they use the correct oil grade (0W-20 for 2018+) and genuine or quality aftermarket parts.

### Q: Is the 1.3L or 1.5L better?
A: The 1.5L (2NR-VE) provides noticeably more power for highway driving and hill climbs, but the 1.3L (1NR-VE) is perfectly adequate for city driving and uses slightly less fuel. Both engines use the same oil and have similar reliability. The 1.5L is worth the extra cost if you frequently drive on highways or carry passengers.

### Q: My Myvi idles roughly with A/C on. Is that normal?
A: A slight vibration increase with A/C on is normal because the compressor adds load. But if the steering wheel or cabin shakes noticeably, check: (1) engine mountings, (2) throttle body cleanliness, (3) spark plugs. Scan with BYKI for any fault codes.
