## Proton's Engine Families

Understanding which engine your Proton uses helps you understand oil requirements:

### 1. Geely-sourced engines (newer models)
- **1.5L Turbo TGDi** — Used in X50, X90, S70
- **1.8L Turbo TGDi** — Used in X70
- Modern, efficient, require quality full synthetic oil
- Oil spec: 5W-30 or 5W-40 (check specific variant)

### 2. VVT engines (mid-range models)
- **1.3L VVT** — Used in Saga (2016+), Iriz 1.3
- **1.6L VVT** — Used in Persona (2016+), Iriz 1.6
- Based on Campro platform but with VVT (Variable Valve Timing)
- Oil spec: 5W-30 (semi or full synthetic)

### 3. Campro engines (older models)
- **1.3L/1.6L Campro** — Used in older Saga, Persona, Waja
- **1.6L Campro CFE (turbo)** — Used in Preve, Suprima S, Exora turbo
- Proton's own engine design
- Oil spec: 5W-30 or 10W-40 (semi-synthetic), 5W-40 for turbo variants

## Common Proton Oil Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using 10W-40 in VVT engines
Older mechanics accustomed to Campro engines may default to 10W-40. The VVT (Variable Valve Timing) system in the Saga VVT, Persona VVT, and Iriz works better with 5W-30 — thinner oil flows faster through VVT solenoids.

### Mistake 2: Using 5W-30 in turbo Proton engines
The Preve, Suprima S, and X70 turbo engines need 5W-40 for adequate turbo bearing protection. 5W-30 is too thin for these engines' turbocharger oil supply.

### Mistake 3: Mixing brands unnecessarily
There's no benefit to mixing brands. Pick one product and stick with it. Mixing Petronas and Shell, for example, won't harm the engine, but using a single product consistently is cleaner.

### Mistake 4: Extending intervals with semi-synthetic
If you're using semi-synthetic 5W-30, don't push past 7,000 km between changes in Malaysian conditions. Semi-synthetic degrades faster than full synthetic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Does Proton manufacture their own oil?
A: No — Proton is connected to Petronas through DRB-HICOM. Proton service centres use Petronas Syntium products as their official oil. This is a business partnership, but Petronas Syntium is genuinely a quality oil brand (Petronas is a fuel technology company with F1 credentials via Mercedes-AMG).

### Q: Can I use 0W-20 in my Proton?
A: No Proton model currently specifies 0W-20. The thinnest Proton recommendation is 5W-30. Using 0W-20 in a Proton engine designed for 5W-30 provides insufficient film thickness, especially under the load conditions these engines experience.

### Q: My Proton Saga uses a lot of oil. Should I switch to thicker oil?
A: If your Saga VVT consumes more than 0.5L per 5,000 km, first check for leaks (valve cover gasket is common). If no external leaks, switching from 5W-30 to 5W-40 may reduce consumption slightly by providing a thicker film. But this should be a deliberate choice, not a default.

### Q: Is full synthetic worth it for a Proton Saga?
A: In pure cost terms, semi-synthetic at RM 80 every 5,000 km vs full synthetic at RM 160 every 10,000 km is roughly equal over the long run. Full synthetic reduces workshop visits and provides better protection. If you can afford the per-change cost, full synthetic is the better choice even for the Saga.

### Q: What oil does Proton use in their factory fill?
A: Proton's factory fill (the oil the car ships with from the factory) is Petronas Syntium — typically 5W-30 for VVT models and 5W-40 for turbo models. Your first oil change at the 1,000 km or 1-month service drains this factory fill.
