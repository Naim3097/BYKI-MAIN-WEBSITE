# Car Idling Rough — Why Your Engine Shakes at Idle & How to Fix It

> **Quick Answer:** Rough idling is usually caused by dirty fuel injectors, worn spark plugs, a faulty idle air control (IAC) valve, vacuum leaks, or a contaminated throttle body. The engine shakes, vibrates, or produces an uneven RPM at idle because one or more cylinders aren't firing smoothly at low speed.

You're sitting at a red light and your car starts shaking. The RPM needle dances up and down. The steering wheel vibrates. The engine sounds like it's struggling to stay alive. That's rough idling — and it's one of the most common engine complaints in Malaysia.

A healthy engine should idle smoothly between 600–800 RPM with minimal vibration. When the idle becomes uneven, it means the engine isn't maintaining consistent combustion at low speed. The causes range from inexpensive fixes (spark plugs) to more involved repairs (vacuum leaks, sensor faults).

## What Does Rough Idle Feel Like?

Rough idle can manifest in several ways:
- **Vibrations** felt through the steering wheel, seat, and dashboard
- **RPM fluctuation** — the tachometer swings between 500–1,000 RPM instead of sitting steady
- **Engine shudder** — the whole car trembles noticeably at traffic lights
- **Near-stalling** — RPM drops dangerously low (below 500) before recovering
- **Inconsistent engine sound** — instead of a smooth hum, you hear uneven stuttering
- **Worse with A/C on** — air conditioning adds engine load, making marginal idle problems obvious

In severe cases, the car may actually stall at idle — especially when you come to a stop after driving.

## 10 Common Causes of Rough Idle

### 1. Dirty or faulty idle air control (IAC) valve
The IAC valve controls how much air bypasses the throttle plate at idle. This is how the engine maintains a consistent idle speed. When it gets clogged with carbon deposits — extremely common in Malaysian driving conditions — it can't regulate airflow properly.

**Symptoms:** Unstable RPM at idle, engine stalls when foot comes off accelerator, idle speed too high or too low.

**Common in:** Proton Wira, Proton Satria, Perodua Kancil — older models with cable-operated throttle.

### 2. Worn spark plugs
At idle, the engine is at its most sensitive. The lean fuel mixture and low RPM make consistent combustion harder. Spark plugs that work fine at highway speed may produce weak or inconsistent sparks at idle, causing roughness.

### 3. Vacuum leaks
Vacuum leaks let unmeasured air into the intake, creating a lean condition. At idle, the throttle is nearly closed, so even a small vacuum leak represents a large proportional change in airflow. This makes vacuum leaks one of the most common causes of rough idle specifically.

**Where to look:** Intake manifold gasket, brake booster hose, PCV valve hose, any rubber hose connected to the intake system.

### 4. Contaminated throttle body
Carbon and oil vapour deposits coat the throttle plate and bore over time. This restricts airflow and causes the plate to stick, leading to erratic idle. Cleaning the throttle body is a common and effective fix.

**Cleaning interval:** Every 30,000–40,000 km, or when rough idle symptoms appear.

### 5. Dirty fuel injectors
Partially clogged injectors deliver uneven amounts of fuel to different cylinders. At idle, where fuel quantities are already minimal, even a slight imbalance causes noticeable roughness.

### 6. Faulty mass airflow (MAF) sensor
An inaccurate MAF sensor causes the ECU to miscalculate fuel injection. At idle, this creates a lean or rich condition that disrupts smooth combustion.

**Quick test:** Some cars idle better with the MAF sensor unplugged. If disconnecting the MAF improves idle quality, the sensor is likely faulty or contaminated.

### 7. EGR valve stuck open
The EGR valve should be closed at idle. If it's stuck open due to carbon buildup, exhaust gas dilutes the intake charge, causing very rough idling, stalling, and sometimes a sulphur smell.

### 8. Low compression in one cylinder
Worn piston rings, a burnt valve, or a leaking head gasket in one cylinder reduces compression in that cylinder. At idle, this cylinder barely fires, causing uneven running. The roughness is usually more noticeable when the engine is cold.

### 9. Failing engine mounts
This isn't an engine combustion problem — the engine may be running fine, but worn engine mounts fail to absorb normal vibrations. The result feels like rough idle because vibrations transfer directly to the cabin.

**How to tell:** If the engine sounds smooth but the car vibrates excessively, check the mounts. Visible movement of the engine when revved in park suggests worn mounts.

**Common in:** Perodua Myvi (2005–2011), Honda City, Proton Persona — models with high mileage.

### 10. Outdated engine software / idle relearn needed
Some modern vehicles need an idle relearn procedure after a battery disconnection, ECU reset, or throttle body cleaning. Without it, the ECU doesn't know the correct idle parameters, causing rough or erratic idle.

## How to Diagnose with BYKI

Rough idle is frustrating because multiple causes produce similar symptoms. A diagnostic scan cuts through the guesswork.

### What BYKI reveals:
- **Misfire codes** (P0300–P0304) — identifies if specific cylinders are misfiring at idle
- **IAC codes** — idle air control circuit malfunction
- **Lean/rich codes** (P0171, P0172) — air-fuel mixture imbalance
- **MAF codes** (P0100–P0104) — airflow sensor faults
- **EGR codes** (P0400–P0409) — exhaust gas recirculation issues
- **Live RPM data** — monitor idle RPM stability in real time

### Diagnosis approach:
1. Connect your Bluetooth OBD-II adapter
2. Open [BYKI](https://www.bykiofficial.com/login)
3. Start with **Health Scan** — look for any stored fault codes
4. Switch to **Live Data** — watch RPM at idle. Normal: steady 650–750 RPM. Rough idle: fluctuating 500–900 RPM
5. Monitor **fuel trims** — large positive values = lean (suspect vacuum leak, MAF). Large negative values = rich (suspect injector, O2 sensor)
6. Check **misfire counts** if available — identifies specific problematic cylinders

## Prevention Tips

1. **Clean the throttle body** every 30,000–40,000 km — a RM 70–140 service that prevents most idle issues
2. **Replace spark plugs on schedule** — weak plugs affect idle first
3. **Inspect vacuum hoses** during services — replace cracked or brittle ones
4. **Use quality fuel** and add fuel system cleaner every 10,000 km
5. **Don't disconnect the battery unnecessarily** — some cars need idle relearn afterward
6. **Address check engine lights promptly** — many idle problems start as fault codes before you feel them

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Why does my car shake at idle but drive smoothly?
A: At idle, the engine produces minimal power and runs at its leanest fuel mixture. Small problems that are masked at higher RPMs (where more fuel and air compensate) become evident at idle. Common culprits are dirty throttle body, worn spark plugs, or vacuum leaks. Engine mounts also play a role — they absorb vibration better at higher speeds.

### Q: Is rough idle dangerous?
A: Rough idle itself isn't dangerous, but it can lead to stalling — which is dangerous if it happens in traffic or at intersections. Additionally, the underlying cause (misfires, lean running) can damage the catalytic converter over time. Diagnose and fix it within a few weeks.

### Q: Why does rough idle get worse when A/C is on?
A: Air conditioning puts significant load on the engine via the compressor belt. A healthy engine compensates by increasing idle speed slightly (the IAC valve opens more). If the IAC is dirty or the engine is already struggling at idle, the added A/C load pushes it over the edge into rough running or stalling. This is very common in Malaysia where A/C runs non-stop.

### Q: Can a rough idle fix itself?
A: Rarely. Some causes (like moisture-related issues after heavy rain) can resolve temporarily, but most rough idle causes — dirty throttle body, worn plugs, vacuum leaks — only get worse over time. A minor rough idle today becomes stalling at traffic lights next month.

### Q: My car rough idles only when cold. Why?
A: When the engine is cold, it runs a richer mixture and the ECU compensates based on the coolant temperature sensor. If spark plugs are weak, they struggle more with the cold, dense air. Vacuum leaks also worsen when rubber contracts in cooler conditions (though Malaysia's "cold" mornings are still above 20°C). Once warm, these marginal components perform adequately — masking the issue.
