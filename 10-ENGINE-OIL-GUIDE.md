# Module 10 — Engine Oil Guide

## 10.1 Why Engine Oil Matters

Engine oil is the lifeblood of your engine. It performs five critical functions:

1. **Lubrication** — reduces friction between moving metal parts (pistons, bearings, camshaft)
2. **Cooling** — carries heat away from combustion zones and bearings
3. **Cleaning** — detergents in the oil suspend contaminants and prevent sludge buildup
4. **Sealing** — helps seal the gap between piston rings and cylinder walls
5. **Protection** — anti-wear additives form a protective film on metal surfaces

Using the wrong oil — or neglecting oil changes — is one of the most common causes of premature engine wear and failure.

## 10.2 Understanding Viscosity

Viscosity is how "thick" or "thin" the oil is — its resistance to flow. Oil viscosity is the single most important specification when choosing engine oil.

### Reading Viscosity Grades

Modern engine oils use a **multi-grade** system — two numbers separated by a "W":

```
  0W-20
  │  │
  │  └── Hot viscosity (operating temp, 100°C)
  └───── Cold viscosity ("W" = Winter, cold-start flow)
```

| Part | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **First number (e.g., 0W, 5W, 10W)** | Cold-start flowability. Lower = flows faster when cold. 0W flows faster than 5W, which flows faster than 10W. |
| **Second number (e.g., 20, 30, 40)** | Oil film thickness at operating temperature (100°C). Higher = thicker film. |

### Common Viscosity Grades in Malaysia

| Grade | Type | Common Use |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **0W-20** | Ultra-low viscosity, full synthetic | Modern fuel-efficient engines (Perodua, Toyota, Honda, Mazda, Hyundai) |
| **5W-30** | Low viscosity, full synthetic | Proton NA, some European, diesel applications |
| **5W-40** | Medium viscosity, full synthetic | European performance, Chinese turbo SUVs |
| **0W-40** | Wide-range, full synthetic | Premium European (BMW, Mercedes, VW), Proton turbo |
| **10W-40** | Standard viscosity, semi-synthetic | Older vehicles, budget applications |
| **15W-40** | Heavy viscosity, mineral | Very old engines, wide tolerances |
| **20W-50** | Extra heavy, mineral | Classic/vintage cars, high-mileage engines with wear |

### Malaysian Climate and Viscosity

In Malaysia's tropical climate (28–38°C ambient), the "W" (winter) number is less critical than in cold countries — we never see sub-zero temperatures. However, the cold-start number still matters because:

- Lower "W" numbers (0W, 5W) provide faster oil flow during the first seconds after starting
- Even in Malaysia, the engine is "cold" relative to its operating temperature when first started
- Faster oil flow at startup = less wear during the critical first 30 seconds of engine operation

## 10.3 Oil Types

### Mineral (Conventional)

- Made from refined crude oil
- Cheapest option
- Adequate for older engines with wider tolerances
- Shorter change intervals (5,000 km)
- Breaking down faster under heat
- **Example:** Syntium 800 15W-40, Syntium 800 20W-50

### Semi-Synthetic (Synthetic Blend)

- A blend of mineral and synthetic base oils
- Better performance than mineral at moderate cost
- Good for everyday driving in older or NA vehicles
- Standard change intervals (7,000–8,000 km)
- **Example:** Syntium 800 10W-40, Syntium 1000 10W-30

### Full Synthetic

- Engineered base oils with advanced additive packages
- Best performance: heat resistance, wear protection, fuel economy
- Required for turbocharged and modern engines
- Longer change intervals (10,000–15,000 km)
- **Example:** Syntium 7000 0W-20, Syntium 5000 CP 5W-30, Syntium 3000 E 5W-40

## 10.4 Understanding Oil Specifications

### API (American Petroleum Institute)

| Rating | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **API SP** | Latest standard (2020+). GDI turbo protection, LSPI prevention. |
| **API SN Plus** | Previous standard. LSPI protection added. |
| **API SN** | Good all-around specification. |
| **API SN/CF** | Dual-rated for petrol and diesel engines. |
| **API SL** | Older standard. Still adequate for pre-2010 vehicles. |

> **Rule:** You can always use a newer API rating than specified. API SP is backward-compatible with SN, SM, SL, etc.

### ILSAC (International Lubricant Specification Advisory Committee)

| Rating | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **ILSAC GF-6A** | Latest. Required by Toyota, Honda, Nissan for 0W-20 applications. |
| **ILSAC GF-5** | Previous standard. Still widely used. |

ILSAC grades apply only to 0W-20 and 5W-20/5W-30 fuel-economy oils. They don't apply to 5W-40 or 0W-40 oils.

### ACEA (Association des Constructeurs Européens d'Automobiles)

| Category | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **ACEA A3/B4** | High-performance petrol and diesel. Required by Mercedes (229.5), BMW (LL-01), VW (502.00). |
| **ACEA C2** | Low-SAPS for DPF protection. Ford diesel applications. |
| **ACEA C3** | Low-SAPS for modern European vehicles with DPF. MB 229.51, BMW LL-04. |
| **ACEA C4** | Ultra-low-SAPS. Renault-specific applications. |
| **ACEA A5/B5** | Fuel-economy focused. Ford petrol applications. |

### OEM Approvals

Major manufacturers have their own specific oil approval standards:

| OEM Approval | Manufacturer | Significance |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| **MB 229.5** | Mercedes-Benz | Required for most modern Mercedes petrol engines |
| **BMW LL-01** | BMW | Standard longlife approval for BMW petrol engines |
| **BMW LL-04** | BMW | Low-SAPS for BMW with DPF |
| **BMW LL-17 FE+** | BMW | Latest fuel economy spec for newer BMW |
| **VW 502.00** | Volkswagen/Audi | Standard VW petrol specification |
| **VW 504.00/507.00** | Volkswagen/Audi | Longlife III specification for VW/Audi service intervals |
| **Ford WSS-M2C913-D** | Ford | Ford petrol longlife specification |
| **Ford WSS-M2C950-A** | Ford | Ford diesel specification (Ranger, Everest) |

> **Rule:** If your vehicle requires a specific OEM approval (e.g., "BMW LL-01"), you should use an oil that lists that exact approval — not just an oil with the same viscosity.

## 10.5 When to Change Oil

| Oil Type | Typical Interval | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Mineral | 5,000 km or 3 months | Whichever comes first |
| Semi-Synthetic | 7,000–8,000 km or 6 months | Most common for older Malaysian vehicles |
| Full Synthetic | 10,000–15,000 km or 6–12 months | Per manufacturer specification |

### Factors That Shorten Oil Life

- **Stop-start city driving** (KL traffic) — oil doesn't reach full operating temperature
- **Short trips** (<10 km) — moisture accumulates in oil
- **Hot ambient temperatures** — Malaysia's climate accelerates oil oxidation
- **Turbocharging** — higher temperatures accelerate oil breakdown
- **Towing or heavy loads** — increased thermal stress
- **Dusty conditions** — contaminants enter faster

> **BYKI's Role:** After a health scan, BYKI assesses your engine's oil stress level. If higher-than-normal thermal stress is detected, the oil recommendation may suggest a higher-tier Syntium grade for better protection.

## 10.6 Transmission Fluid Guide

Your automatic transmission also requires specific fluid:

| Type | Application | Change Interval |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **ATF (Automatic Transmission Fluid)** | Conventional automatics (4AT, 6AT, 8AT, 9AT) | 40,000–60,000 km |
| **CVTF (CVT Fluid)** | Continuously Variable Transmissions (Perodua, Toyota, Nissan, Honda) | 40,000–80,000 km |
| **DCTF (Dual-Clutch Fluid)** | Wet dual-clutch transmissions (VW DSG, Proton 7DCT, Hyundai/Kia 7DCT) | 40,000–60,000 km |
| **Manual Transmission Oil** | Manual gearboxes | 60,000–80,000 km |

> **Never mix transmission fluid types.** Using ATF in a CVT (or vice versa) can cause transmission damage. Always use the correct fluid type for your transmission.
